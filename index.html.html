UDUPI -A Spiritual and Coastal Paradise

Introduction

Udupi is a charming coastal town in Karnataka,known for its rich cultural heritage,ancient temples and delicious sea foods.It is home to the famous Sri Krishna Temple,a significant pilgrimage site that attracts devotees from all over.Beyond its spiritual significance,Udupi also offers beautiful beaches like Malpe,Maravanthe and Kaup,where visitors can enjoy breathtaking sunsets and peaceful surroundings.Whether you seek spirituality,nature or food,Udupi provides a perfect blend of all three,making it a must-visit destination.

Famous WaterFalls:

Koodlu Theertha Falls Kudlu Theertha Falls is located amid jungles of Western Ghats in Udupi district and recognized as the first water fall of river Sita. The water falls from a height of 126 feet directly to the pond. It is believed that this pond is holy because many sages performed penance here many years ago.
Arbi Falls Located just 7 km from Udupi and 2.8 km from Manipal, Arbi Falls is a picturesque retreat, perfect for those seeking a serene escape into nature. Nestled in the tranquil village of Badagabettu, this waterfall comes alive during the monsoon season, offering a captivating display of cascading water amidst lush greenery.
Jomlu Theertha Falls Jomlu Teertha is a beautiful waterfall located near the village of Hebri in Udupi, Karnatak. Surrounded by lush green forests and rolling hills, the waterfall offers a refreshing and picturesque setting, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.
Belkal Theertha Falls The Belkal Theertha Waterfall lies near Kollur and Madur and it is situated in the lush green forests of Mookambika Wildlife Sanctuary. The waterfall is also called as Govinda Theertha. The water is falling 300 feet down from a mountain called Korshi Betta, which is an extension of Kodachadri.

Famous Trekking Places:

Kodachadri Trek The Kodachadri hill is a Natural Heritage Site, which is part of the Western Ghats and forms a lovely backdrop to the famous Kollur Mookambika Temple. Famed for its glorious sunrises and sunsets, this hill range is part of the Mookambika Temple Nature Reserve.
Kudlu Theertha Trek Most tourists visit Agumbe to trek through its forests or to visit the waterfalls. Trekking to the Kudlu Theertha Falls or Nishani Gudda offers a once in a lifetime experience. (But also beware of the leeches that infest the forests.) The high points of the treks are the visual treats that Agumbe offers.
Belkal Theertha trek Reaching Belkal Theertha involves a trek through rugged trails, bamboo groves, and streams, offering a thrilling experience for adventurers. The journey is as rewarding as the destination, with abundant opportunities to observe wildlife and rare flora.
